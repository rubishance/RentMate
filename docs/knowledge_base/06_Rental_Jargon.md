# Hebrew Rental Market Jargon (מילון מונחי שכירות)

This glossary contains essential professional terminology (Jargon) used in the Israeli real estate and rental market. The bot should understand these terms to sound like a local expert.

## Financial & Payments
- **שק (Check)**: Standard payment method. Often post-dated (צ'קים דחויים).
- **הוראת קבע (Hora'at Keva)**: Standing order / Direct debit. Common for utility bills.
- **מזומן (Mezuman)**: Cash. Less common for rent due to documentation needs, but used for deposits.
- **ערבות בנקאית (Aravut Bankait)**: Bank Guarantee. Money locked in the tenant's bank account in favor of the landlord. High security.
- **שטר חוב (Shtar Chov)**: Promissory Note. A legal document where the tenant commits to pay X amount if they breach contract.
- **צ'ק ביטחון (Check Bitachon)**: Security Check. Undated check kept by the landlord, usually for utilities or damages.
- **אופציה (Optzia)**: Option to renew. A clause allowing the tenant to extend the lease for another year under specific terms.
- **גרייס (Grace)**: Grace period. Free rent days (usually for setup/moving in).

## Taxes & Government
- **ארנונה (Arnona)**: Municipal Property Tax. Tenant usually pays this unless stated otherwise.
- **ועד בית (Vaad Bayit)**: Building Committee Fee / HOA. Covers cleaning, elevator, lights in public areas.
- **טאבו (Tabu)**: Land Registry. The official government record of property ownership.
- **מס רכישה (Mas Rechisha)**: Purchase Tax (relevant for owners).
- **מס שבח (Mas Shevach)**: Capital Gains Tax (relevant for owners selling).
- **פטור ממס (Ptor Mi-Mas)**: Tax Exemption. Specifically the ~5,600 NIS exemption for residential rental income.
- **מסלול 10% (Maslul 10%)**: The flat tax track for rental income above the exemption limit.

## Apartment Features
- **ממ"ד (Mamad)**: Reinforced Security Room (Merkhav Mugan Dirati). Mandatory in new builds. Serves as a bedroom/office.
- **מרפסת שמש (Mirpeset Shemesh)**: Sun Balcony.
- **חנייה בטאבו (Chanaya Be-Tabu)**: Private parking spot listed in the deed.
- **מחסן (Machsan)**: Private storage unit.
- **מרוהטת (Murohetet)**: Furnished.
- **דוד שמש (Dud Shemesh)**: Solar Water Heater. Essential for saving electricity in Israel.
- **יונקרס (Junkers)**: Gas heating system for water/home (common in Jerusalem/cold areas).
- **סורגים (Soragim)**: Window bars (for security or child safety).

## Contract Terms
- **הפרה יסודית (Hafara Yesodit)**: Fundamental Breach. A serious contract violation (e.g., non-payment) allowing immediate termination.
- **כוח עליון (Koach Elyon)**: Force Majeure. Unforeseeable circumstances (war, earthquake).
- **פינוי מושכר (Pinui Muschar)**: Eviction of the rented property.
- **שוכר חלופי (Socher Chalufi)**: Alternative Tenant. Someone who takes over the lease if the original tenant leaves early.
- **בלאי סביר (Blai Savir)**: Reasonable Wear and Tear. Damage that happens naturally over time (landlord's responsibility).

## Maintenance & Issues
- **רטיבות (Retivut)**: Moisture/Mold issues. A very common dispute in Israel.
- **פיצוץ בצנרת (Pitzutz Be-Tzaneret)**: Burst pipe. Landlord responsibility unless caused by tenant negligence.
- **הדברה (Hadbara)**: Pest control.
- **דוד התפוצץ (Dud Hitpotzetz)**: Boiler exploded.

## Slang / Street Talk
- **דירת שותפים (Dirat Shutafim)**: Roommate apartment.
- **יחידת דיור (Yechidat Diur)**: Housing Unit. Often a subdivided part of a villa owner's basement.
- **דמי תיווך (Dmei Tivuch)**: Brokerage fee. Usually one month's rent + VAT.
- **מציאה (Metzia)**: A bargain / Great deal.
- **חורבה (Churva)**: A dump / Ruin (slang for bad apartment).
