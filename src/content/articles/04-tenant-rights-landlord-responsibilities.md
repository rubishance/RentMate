### Landlord's Legal Obligations

As a landlord in Israel, you have several mandatory responsibilities:

#### 1. **Habitable Conditions**
- Provide a safe, clean, and livable property
- Ensure all utilities function properly (water, electricity, heating)
- Address structural issues and safety hazards
- Maintain common areas in multi-unit buildings

#### 2. **Repairs and Maintenance**
- **Landlord pays for:** Structural repairs, plumbing, electrical systems, heating/cooling systems, roof leaks
- **Tenant pays for:** Minor repairs, light bulbs, cleaning, damage caused by tenant

#### 3. **Privacy Rights**
- Give **24-48 hours notice** before entering the property
- Only enter for legitimate reasons (repairs, inspections, showings)
- Cannot harass or disturb tenant's peaceful enjoyment

#### 4. **Security Deposit (Pitzuim)**
- Hold deposit in trust (cannot use for personal purposes)
- Return within **30 days** after lease ends
- Can deduct only for actual damages (not normal wear and tear)
- Must provide itemized list of deductions
