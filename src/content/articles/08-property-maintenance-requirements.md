### Landlord's Legal Duty

Israeli law requires landlords to maintain rental properties in **habitable condition** throughout the lease term. This is a non-negotiable obligation that cannot be waived by contract.

### What "Habitable" Means

A property must have:

âœ… Heating in winter (radiators, AC, or equivalent)  
âœ… Weatherproof structure (no leaks, broken windows)  
âœ… Secure locks on doors and windows  
âœ… Pest-free environment (landlord must address infestations)  
âœ… Safe structure (no collapsing ceilings, dangerous stairs)

### Landlord vs. Tenant Responsibilities

| Repair Type | Who Pays |
|-------------|----------|
| **Structural issues** (foundation, walls, roof) | Landlord |
| **Major systems** (plumbing, electrical, heating) | Landlord |
| **Appliances** (if provided by landlord) | Landlord |
| **Common areas** (stairs, elevator, entrance) | Landlord |
| **Pest control** (unless caused by tenant) | Landlord |
| **Minor repairs** (light bulbs, cabinet handles) | Tenant |
| **Cleaning** (regular upkeep) | Tenant |
| **Damage caused by tenant** (broken tiles, holes) | Tenant |

### Response Time Requirements

While not strictly defined by law, courts expect:

- **Emergency repairs** (no heat in winter, major leak, no electricity): **24-48 hours**
- **Urgent repairs** (broken toilet, minor leak): **3-7 days**
- **Non-urgent repairs** (cosmetic issues, minor inconveniences): **14-30 days**

### How to Handle Maintenance Requests

#### For Landlords:

1. **Acknowledge immediately** - Respond within 24 hours
2. **Assess urgency** - Prioritize health/safety issues
3. **Communicate timeline** - When will repair happen?
4. **Hire qualified professionals** - Don't cut corners
5. **Follow up** - Confirm repair completed satisfactorily
6. **Document everything** - Photos, invoices, communications

#### For Tenants:

1. **Report in writing** - Email or text (creates record)
2. **Be specific** - Describe problem clearly with photos
3. **State urgency** - Is it emergency or can it wait?
4. **Allow access** - Coordinate repair appointments
5. **Follow up** - If no response in reasonable time
6. **Know your rights** - Can withhold rent in extreme cases

### When Tenants Can Withhold Rent

In **extreme cases** where landlord refuses to make essential repairs:
- Tenant can deposit rent in escrow account
- Must notify landlord in writing first
- Only for serious habitability issues (no heat, no water)
- Tenant should seek legal advice before withholding

### Common Disputes

**Issue:** Tenant damages property, landlord wants immediate payment  
**Solution:** Landlord must repair first, then deduct from deposit

**Issue:** Landlord delays non-emergency repairs for months  
**Solution:** Tenant can hire contractor and deduct from rent (with proper notice)

**Issue:** Unclear who pays for appliance repair  
**Solution:** If appliance came with property, landlord pays
