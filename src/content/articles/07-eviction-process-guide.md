## English

### Important: You Cannot Self-Evict

In Israel, landlords **CANNOT** evict tenants without a court order. Actions like changing locks, shutting off utilities, or physically removing belongings are **illegal** and can result in criminal charges.

### Valid Grounds for Eviction

Courts will grant eviction orders for:

âœ… **Non-payment of rent** (most common)  
âœ… **Breach of contract** (unauthorized subletting, illegal activity)  
âœ… **Property damage** beyond normal wear  
âœ… **Nuisance behavior** (excessive noise, harassment)  
âœ… **End of lease term** (if tenant refuses to leave)  
âœ… **Landlord needs property** for personal use (limited circumstances)

### The Legal Process

#### Step 1: Written Notice (7-30 days)
- Send formal demand letter
- State specific violation
- Give reasonable time to remedy (pay rent, stop behavior)
- Send via registered mail for proof

#### Step 2: File Court Claim
- Submit to **Small Claims Court** (up to â‚ª35,000) or **Magistrate Court**
- Include: lease agreement, payment records, correspondence
- Pay court fees (â‚ª500-2,000)
- Serve tenant with court summons

#### Step 3: Court Hearing
- Both parties present evidence
- Tenant can defend or negotiate
- Judge may order mediation first
- Typical timeline: 2-6 months

#### Step 4: Court Order
If landlord wins:
- Judge issues **eviction order**
- Tenant given 30-90 days to vacate
- Order specifies exact date

#### Step 5: Enforcement
If tenant doesn't leave:
- File with **Execution Office** (×”×•×¦××” ×œ×¤×•×¢×œ)
- Bailiff schedules eviction date
- Police may assist if needed
- Landlord cannot be present during physical eviction

### Timeline Expectations

- **Notice period:** 7-30 days
- **Court filing to hearing:** 1-3 months
- **Hearing to verdict:** 1-2 months
- **Verdict to eviction:** 1-3 months
- **Total:** 4-9 months on average

### Tenant Defenses

Tenants can fight eviction by proving:
- Rent was paid (show receipts)
- Landlord didn't maintain property
- Eviction is retaliatory (tenant complained about repairs)
- Landlord violated contract first
- Hardship circumstances (illness, children)

### Costs Involved

**Landlord pays:**
- Court filing fees: â‚ª500-2,000
- Attorney fees: â‚ª5,000-15,000 (optional but recommended)
- Bailiff fees: â‚ª1,000-3,000
- Lost rent during process

**Winner may recover costs** from losing party.

### Avoiding Eviction (For Landlords)

ğŸ† **Screen tenants carefully** - Check references, employment  
ğŸ† **Clear lease terms** - Document everything  
ğŸ† **Address issues early** - Don't wait months  
ğŸ† **Communicate in writing** - Creates evidence trail  
ğŸ† **Offer payment plans** - May resolve faster than court

### Avoiding Eviction (For Tenants)

ğŸ† **Pay rent on time** - Set up automatic transfers  
ğŸ† **Document everything** - Keep receipts, emails  
ğŸ† **Communicate problems** - Don't ignore landlord  
ğŸ† **Know your rights** - Landlord can't self-evict  
ğŸ† **Seek legal aid** - Free services available

### Special Circumstances

**Protected Tenants:**
- Elderly (65+) may get extended timelines
- Families with young children receive consideration
- Disabled tenants have additional protections

**COVID-19 Impact:**
- Courts were backlogged 6-12 months
- Some eviction moratoriums applied
- Check current regulations
